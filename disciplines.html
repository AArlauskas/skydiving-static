Skydiving disciplines

Formation Skydiving

FS or Formation Skydiving is the discipline you will start with after you completed the initial course and some more solo jumps. It is basically jumping together with one or more other fellow skydivers in a belly-to-earth position holding on to each other on prebriefed 'grips'. On a competition level it is subdivided in 4-,8- and 16-way sequentials. The main item on world championships is the 4-way sequential whereby a group of four skydivers has to complete a sequence of predefined 'figures' (different grips) in a set timeframe (usually 35sec starting from the exit). The sequence has to be repeated as much as possible. The belgian team nmp-pch hayabusa is the undisputed world champion since 2014 in this discipline.

Bigways are organised on rare occasions and involve a 'big' number of skydivers. The current world record is a formation of 400 skydivers (400-way), set in Thailand in April 2013.

Free fly

Freefly is the discipline where other than belly-to-earth body positions are used. The two main positions of freefly are sitfly (up to standup) and headdown. This discipline belongs more to the fun category, however there are also VFS (Vertical Formation Skydiving) competitions similar to FS but then with your body in a 'vertical' position.

Tracking / tracing

Tracking and tracing are disciplines whereby your body position (belly-to-earth or back-to-earth) allows a horizontal movement. Feels like you are actually 'flying'!

Wingsuit discipline

Wingsuit is the ultimate way of tracking. Aided by a suit that has the shape of a wing, long horizontal distances can be covered. The descent rate is significantly lower so you will be up much longer compared with a normal skydive.

Canopy formation

In CFS or Canopy Formation Skydiving, you guessed it by now, parachutists are flying in formation under their canopy (parachute) with physical contact between parachutists and canopies (docks). There are, similar to FS, sequential competitions where multiple docks are made on different positions (slots).

Swooping

Swooping is a very spectacular way of landing under parachute. Parachutists build up speed which they then dissipate during the landingflare. Is is divided in three subparts. Accuracy, Speed and Distance. After passing the gate (between two cones on the ground), they trail their feet in the water of the swoop-pond and then touch down as close as possible to a square meter target (accuracy).

In speed, a second gate has to passed as shortly as possible after passing the first gate. In distance the goal is to touch down as far as possible after the gate. Very small and fast parachutes are used for this discipline. For very experienced skydivers only!
